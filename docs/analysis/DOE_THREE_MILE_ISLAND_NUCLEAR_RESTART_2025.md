## The Plant: Crane Clean Energy Center

### Historical Context

**Three Mile Island** is synonymous with nuclear energy in America - both its promise and its challenges.

**The Site**:
- **Location**: Susquehanna River, Londonderry Township, Dauphin County, Pennsylvania
- **Original Build**: Two pressurized water reactors (Unit 1 and Unit 2)
- **Unit 2**: Site of the infamous **1979 partial meltdown** - worst nuclear accident in U.S. history
- **Unit 2 Status**: Permanently shut down after accident, never restarted
- **Unit 1**: Operated successfully from 1974-2019 with excellent safety record

### Unit 1 Shutdown (2019)

**Why it closed**:
- **Financial challenges**: Could not compete with cheap natural gas and renewables
- **Market structure**: PJM Interconnection capacity market did not adequately value baseload reliability
- **No technical issues**: Plant was safe and operational
- **No full decommissioning**: Maintained in cold shutdown, preserving restart option

**This was purely economic, not technical - making restart feasible.**

### The Restart Plan

**New Name**: Crane Clean Energy Center (distancing from Three Mile Island legacy)

**Technical Details**:
- **Reactor Type**: Pressurized Water Reactor (PWR)
- **Capacity**: 835 megawatts (MW)
- **Fuel**: Low-enriched uranium
- **Expected Output**: Baseload power for ~800,000 homes
- **Capacity Factor**: Expected >90% (nuclear typical)

**Timeline**:
- **2024**: Constellation announces restart plans with Microsoft partnership
- **Late 2025**: DOE closes $1 billion loan
- **2025-2027**: Licensing reviews, equipment upgrades, testing
- **2027**: Projected restart (pending NRC approval)

**Required Work**:
- Regulatory approvals from U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission (NRC)
- Equipment inspections and upgrades (5+ years of cold shutdown)
- Safety system testing and certification
- Workforce training and hiring
- Grid interconnection preparations
